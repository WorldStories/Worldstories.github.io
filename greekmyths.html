Greek Myths

Greek mythology, as in other ancient cultures, was used as a means to explain the environment in which people lived, natural phenomena and the passing of time. Myths were also intricately connected to religion in the Greek world and explained the origin and lives of the gods, life and death and gave advice. Myths were both familar and popular with a wide section of Greek society through their common representation in art, for example in sculpture or scenes painted on pottery.
Here are three well known Greek myths:

Persephone and Hades
Persephone is captured by Hades and forced to become goddess of the underworld for half the year....

The Golden Apple of Discord
Eris causes Hera, Athena and Aphrodite to argue over who is the most beautiful....

The Trojan Horse
Odysseus sneaks into the city of Troy inside a large wooden horse....
